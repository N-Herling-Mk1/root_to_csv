ABOUT — the toolkit in one page

What to run, what the scan tells you, and where every branch ends up. Click a number.

HOW TO USE — TWO RUNS
(1) python3 scan.py file.root → produces canonical.parquet + manifest.json + manifest.txt
(2) python3 convert.py file.root (quick)  or   convert.py --from-scan DIR → produces <name>_flat.csv

THE RUNS

Two python files, two layers. scan.py reads the ROOT file once and emits the census; convert.py produces the flat CSV — directly (quick), or from an edited census (from-scan).

WHAT THE SCAN CONVEYS — THE SIX BINS

Every branch lands in exactly one bin. The glyphs are the shape signatures: rows are events, squares are values. Cool → hot with structural difficulty. Full verbose cards: SIX BINS.

HOW THE BINS BECOME CSV — THE FATE MAP

Pass 2 sends each bin to its destination. Solid lines are the primary fate; faint dashes are the parquet safety copies. Nothing is lost silently — what skips the CSV is either preserved in parquet or named in the manifest.
